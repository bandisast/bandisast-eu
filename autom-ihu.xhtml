The Alexander Technological Educational Institute of Thessaloniki was originally founded in 1970 and officially opened in 1974. In 1983, TEIs were officially established as part of Greece's system of tertiary education. In 2001, due to the Bologna Process, degrees from TEIs were recognized as equivalent to those from "standard" universities. In practice, the main difference between a TEI and a "standard" university is that the former is more focused on applied knowledge, while the latter is more focused on theoretical knowledge. In other countries, similar institutions are called polytechnics.

The Department of Automation Engineering was established in 1989, with its main fields of study being Automation, Control Systems, and Systems Engineering. Upon completing their studies, the department's graduates are legally recognised as Automation Engineers and are awarded a Bachelor's degree in Automation Engineering (EQF level 6). This degree can be recognised across the European Union (and other countries), according to the European Qualifications Framework.

In 2019, the International Hellenic University was merged with the Alexander TEI of Thessaloniki, the TEI of Central Macedonia, and the TEI of Eastern Macedonia and Thrace. During this process, the Department of Automation Engineering was merged with the Department of Automotive Engineering into the "Department of Industrial Engineering & Management". The previous two departments (Automation Engineering and Automative Engineering) will remain in hybrid operation until all current students have completed their studies (i.e. until 2027). In practice, this means that the old curricula were matched with the new curriculum so that every old course had a corresponding unit (with approximately the same material) in the new department.
